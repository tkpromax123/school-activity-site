Enhancing Physical Activity in Our School

Promoting health, learning, and wellbeing through daily movement.

Daily Guideline: 30 Minutes of Movement

Minimum: Aim for at least 30 minutes of moderate exercise per day. You can split this across the day (e.g., 3 × 10-minute bursts) — it all adds up.

Physical activity enhances students’ ability to learn and supports mental health and wellbeing. Regular moderate exercise builds a healthy lifestyle that continues into adulthood.

  • Moderate intensity = breathing faster but still able to talk.
  • Mix activities you enjoy: walking, cycling, sport, skipping, bodyweight circuits.
  • Pair movement with routines: before school, recess/lunch, after school.

Importance of Physical Activity

For school-aged students, regular movement supports not only physical health but also cognitive performance and emotional wellbeing. Research shows daily activity improves concentration, mood, and academic achievement.

  • Improves cardiovascular health and muscular strength.
  • Enhances concentration and learning capacity.
  • Reduces stress and supports emotional resilience.
  • Promotes healthy habits that extend into adulthood.

Our Community Context

Community refers to a group of people with shared characteristics or interests (e.g., school community, Australian community, sports community, friendship community). Understanding our school community helps us design activity that is inclusive and engaging.

  • We have access to information internally and can survey students regularly.
  • We can observe activity patterns across year levels and during breaks.
  • We have facilities (indoor/outdoor spaces) that can support movement-friendly culture.

Current Observations in Our School

  • Many students prioritise academic work over regular movement.
  • Opportunities for informal activity outside PE classes are limited.
  • Sports participation varies significantly between year levels.
  • Break times are often spent sitting rather than moving.

    • Benefits for the School Community

    • Improved focus and classroom behaviour.
    • Higher participation in school life and extracurriculars.
    • Stronger social connections and sense of belonging.
    • Lower absenteeism from health-related issues.

    Strategies to Encourage Activity

    To foster a more active school culture, we can:

    • Encourage active play during recess and lunch periods.
    • Expand the range of activities offered in PE.
    • Provide access to indoor/outdoor spaces during free periods.
    • Integrate movement-based strategies into classroom lessons.
    • Educate on long-term health benefits of regular activity.
